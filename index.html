How it works

Part 1 — The Math Engine. It reads your historical results and, for football, builds an attack strength and defence strength for every team (goals scored/conceded versus league average, recent matches weighted more heavily). For any fixture it computes expected goals for each side, then uses the Poisson distribution to price every scoreline (0-0, 1-0, 2-1 …). Summing those cells gives Win / Draw / Loss, Over/Under, Both Teams To Score and correct-score probabilities. Scorer odds come from each player's share of his team's goals applied to today's expected goals. Basketball uses offensive/defensive point ratings and a normal distribution over the margin.

Part 2 — The AI Writer. The engine's raw numbers are handed to an AI which writes the pre-match report. Without an API key a built-in template writer does this; with a Claude key the numbers go to Claude for a fluent analysis of form, head-to-head, injuries and a final verdict.

Live data: with an API-Football key the app loads real fixtures and top scorers for a league season and pulls injury news for both teams on every analysis. The injury list is shown and fed to the AI writer, though the probability engine itself is results-based and does not discount for absences.

Honest limits: lineups, motivation and weather aren't modelled yet, and no model can predict sport reliably. Treat outputs as probabilities, not certainties.
